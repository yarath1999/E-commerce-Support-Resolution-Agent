MARKETPLACE POLICY — THIRD‑PARTY SELLERS vs FIRST‑PARTY (SYNTHETIC SAMPLE)
Effective date: 2026-03-30
Applies to: Orders placed on the marketplace platform that may be sold by (a) the platform/brand (first‑party) or (b) independent sellers (third‑party).

1) OVERVIEW: WHO HANDLES WHAT
1.1 First‑party listings
If the order confirmation states “Sold by Platform” or “Sold by Brand,” then the Global First‑Party Store Policies apply, plus any Regional Addendum.

1.2 Third‑party seller listings
If the order confirmation states “Sold by [Seller Name],” the seller is the merchant of record.
This means:
- The seller may set return rules within platform limits.
- The platform may still provide a minimum buyer protection standard.
If seller rules conflict with platform buyer protection, the platform standard and local law prevail.

2) RETURNS & REFUNDS (MARKETPLACE)
2.1 Platform minimum standard
Unless prohibited by law or clearly disclosed at checkout, third‑party sellers must follow these minimum standards:
- Provide a return option for non‑final-sale goods within at least 14 days of delivery (region may vary).
- Provide refunds for items that arrive damaged, defective, or not as described.
- Provide a clear description of return shipping responsibility.

2.2 Final sale in the marketplace
Sellers may mark items as “final sale” only if:
- The listing clearly discloses final sale before purchase, and
- The item category is eligible for final sale under local law.
Even for final sale items, the buyer may still receive remedies for:
- Damage in transit, incorrect item, counterfeit, or misrepresentation.

2.3 Hygiene items
For hygiene or sealed goods, the platform may restrict returns once opened.
Seller must disclose whether the item is sealed and what “opened” means.
Condition examples:
- If seal is intact: return may be accepted within the return window.
- If seal is broken: return may be denied except for verified defect or wrong item.

2.4 Perishables
Perishables are typically non‑returnable.
Platform buyer protection may provide refund/replacement if:
- The issue is reported within 24–48 hours of delivery, and
- The buyer provides clear photos and order details.
Sellers may be required to use platform-approved shipping methods for perishables.

2.5 Opened items (non-hygiene)
Sellers may allow opened returns for certain categories (e.g., apparel) but may apply condition-based deductions.
Platform limits:
- Deductions must be reasonable and tied to diminished value.
- The seller must explain deductions and, where required, provide evidence.

2.6 Return shipping labels
Return shipping responsibility depends on issue type:
- Seller fault (damaged, incorrect, defective, not as described): seller pays return shipping or provides prepaid label.
- Buyer remorse (changed mind, wrong size): buyer may pay return shipping.
Platform may require sellers to provide prepaid labels for certain regions or categories.

2.7 Refund timing
Refunds are typically issued after the returned item is delivered to the seller or a platform warehouse and inspected.
Condition: If the platform uses “instant refunds,” it may later reverse the refund if the return is incomplete or fraudulent.

3) CANCELLATIONS (MARKETPLACE)
3.1 Buyer-initiated cancellations
Cancellations are allowed only before the seller confirms shipment.
Conditions:
- Once a seller marks the order shipped or a label is created, cancellation may be blocked.
- If blocked, the buyer may need to return the item after delivery.

3.2 Seller-initiated cancellations
Sellers may cancel if out of stock, unable to ship to the address, or suspected fraud.
Platform action:
- Repeated seller cancellations may affect seller performance.
- Buyer receives a full refund; coupon restoration depends on promotion terms.

4) SHIPPING & DELIVERY
4.1 Delivery estimates
Marketplace delivery dates are estimates based on seller handling time and carrier transit.
Sellers must disclose handling time (e.g., “ships in 2 business days”).

4.2 Delays
If a shipment is late:
- Buyer should first check tracking and messaging.
- Seller must respond within a platform-defined time window.
Platform may grant remedies (credit/refund) if:
- The item does not arrive by the latest estimated date plus a grace period, and
- Tracking indicates a carrier exception or lack of movement.

4.3 Lost packages
A package may be treated as lost if:
- There is no tracking movement for 7+ days, or
- Tracking indicates delivery but the buyer reports non‑receipt within 48 hours.
Conditions:
- Buyer must confirm address, check safe locations, and cooperate with investigation.
- For high-value items, platform may request a signed statement; some regions may require a police report reference.
Resolution:
- Refund or reshipment depending on inventory and seller capability.
If tracking proves correct delivery to the provided address (GPS/photo/signature where available), the claim may be denied.

5) PROMOTIONS & COUPON RULES (MARKETPLACE)
5.1 Platform coupons vs seller coupons
- Platform coupons are funded by the platform and apply under platform terms.
- Seller coupons are funded by the seller and apply only to the seller’s eligible items.

5.2 One coupon per order and stacking
Unless explicitly stated, coupon stacking is not allowed.
Some campaigns allow stacking with “free shipping” offers; if allowed, it will be stated in the promotion terms.

5.3 Minimum spend and partial returns
If a coupon required a minimum spend and you return items that reduce the net spend below the threshold:
- The coupon discount may be prorated or removed.
- The refund will reflect the updated net payable amount.
Condition: If the platform provided a “bonus credit” (e.g., $10 off $50), the bonus may be forfeited when the threshold is no longer met.

5.4 Coupon abuse
The platform may limit or remove promotions for:
- Multiple-account abuse, automated redemption, or resale behavior.
- Chargeback patterns or suspicious payment activity.
If an order is cancelled for suspected abuse, the buyer may be asked to use an alternative payment method or complete identity verification.

6) DISPUTES: DAMAGED OR INCORRECT ITEMS
6.1 Damaged item
Report within 7 days of delivery (or sooner for perishables).
Evidence conditions:
- Photos of item, packaging, and shipping label.
- For electronics, a short video showing the issue may be requested.
Remedies:
- Replacement, partial refund, or full refund.
If the buyer requests replacement, the seller may require return of the damaged unit.

6.2 Incorrect item
Report within 14 days of delivery.
Conditions:
- Buyer must not use the item.
- Provide photos and the packing slip if available.
Remedies:
- Correct item shipped, and prepaid label for return of incorrect item, OR
- Full refund if replacement is unavailable.

6.3 Not as described / counterfeit
If an item is materially different from the listing or suspected counterfeit:
- Buyer must report promptly and provide photos.
- Platform may require return to a secure authentication center.
If confirmed counterfeit, the buyer receives a full refund and may not be required to return the item, depending on local rules.

7) REGIONAL DIFFERENCES
The marketplace must comply with local consumer laws.
Examples of how regional rules can affect marketplace outcomes:
- EU: withdrawal rights may apply to eligible goods, but sealed hygiene goods may be excluded once unsealed.
- India: category-specific return windows and “replacement only” rules may apply.
- US: state restrictions can affect shipping of certain goods.
When in doubt, the platform applies the rule set associated with the delivery destination.

8) CUSTOMER SUPPORT CHANNELS
First‑party orders: contact platform support or brand support as shown on the invoice.
Third‑party orders: message the seller first; escalate to platform support if the seller does not respond within the platform time window or if the issue is covered by buyer protection.
